# Academic Paper Structure Guidelines

This document provides comprehensive guidelines for structuring academic papers.

## Standard Paper Structure

### 1. Abstract (150-250 words)

**Purpose:** Summarize the entire paper concisely.

**Components:**
- Background/Context (1 sentence)
- Problem statement (1 sentence)
- Methodology (1-2 sentences)
- Key findings (2-3 sentences)
- Conclusion/Implications (1 sentence)

**Writing Tips:**
- Write in past tense
- No citations
- Standalone - should make sense without reading the paper
- Use specific numbers when possible

---

### 2. Introduction (10-15% of paper length)

**Purpose:** Establish context and present research question.

**Structure:**
1. **Opening** - Hook the reader with context
2. **Background** - Establish research domain
3. **Problem Statement** - Identify the gap
4. **Research Questions** - State what you investigate
5. **Significance** - Why this matters
6. **Outline** - Brief roadmap (optional)

**Key Elements:**
- Move from general to specific
- Clear thesis statement
- Justification for research
- Preview of methodology

---

### 3. Literature Review (20-25% of paper length)

**Purpose:** Establish theoretical foundation and research gap.

**Organization Approaches:**
- **Chronological** - Historical development
- **Thematic** - By topic or theme
- **Methodological** - By research methods used
- **Geographical** - By region (if applicable)

**Structure:**
1. Overview of research area
2. Major theoretical frameworks
3. Previous empirical studies
4. Gaps in existing research
5. How your study addresses gaps

**Writing Guidelines:**
- Synthesize, don't just summarize
- Identify patterns and contradictions
- Critically evaluate sources
- Connect to your research questions

---

### 4. Methodology (15-20% of paper length)

**Purpose:** Explain how research was conducted.

**Components:**

#### Research Design
- Type of research (quantitative/qualitative/mixed)
- Rationale for design choice

#### Data Collection
- Sample/population description
- Sampling method
- Data collection instruments
- Procedure details

#### Data Analysis
- Analytical methods
- Software/tools used
- Statistical tests (if applicable)

#### Ethics
- Ethical considerations
- Approval details

---

### 5. Results (15-20% of paper length)

**Purpose:** Present findings without interpretation.

**Structure:**
1. Overview of results
2. Main findings (organized by research question)
3. Supporting data (tables, figures)
4. Additional findings

**Guidelines:**
- Present facts only
- Use past tense
- Reference all tables/figures
- Don't discuss implications here

---

### 6. Discussion (20-25% of paper length)

**Purpose:** Interpret results and connect to literature.

**Structure:**
1. Summary of key findings
2. Interpretation of results
3. Comparison with previous research
4. Theoretical implications
5. Practical implications
6. Limitations
7. Future research directions

**Key Elements:**
- Explain what results mean
- Acknowledge limitations honestly
- Connect back to literature review
- Discuss unexpected findings

---

### 7. Conclusion (5-10% of paper length)

**Purpose:** Synthesize and provide closure.

**Components:**
1. Restate main findings (briefly)
2. Answer research questions
3. Theoretical contributions
4. Practical recommendations
5. Final thoughts

**Important:**
- No new information
- Don't just repeat abstract
- End with impact statement

---

### 8. References

**Requirements:**
- Follow chosen citation style consistently
- Include all cited sources
- Verify accuracy of citations
- Sort according to style rules

---

## Paper Length Guidelines

| Section | Short (3000w) | Medium (6000w) | Long (10000w) |
|---------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| Abstract | 150 | 200 | 250 |
| Introduction | 300-450 | 600-900 | 1000-1500 |
| Literature Review | 600-750 | 1200-1500 | 2000-2500 |
| Methodology | 450-600 | 900-1200 | 1500-2000 |
| Results | 450-600 | 900-1200 | 1500-2000 |
| Discussion | 600-750 | 1200-1500 | 2000-2500 |
| Conclusion | 150-300 | 300-600 | 500-1000 |

## Quality Checklist

### Overall Structure
- [ ] Clear logical flow between sections
- [ ] Each section serves its purpose
- [ ] Appropriate length distribution
- [ ] Coherent argument throughout

### Abstract
- [ ] Self-contained
- [ ] All key components included
- [ ] Within word limit
- [ ] No citations

### Introduction
- [ ] Clear research question
- [ ] Sufficient background
- [ ] Justified significance
- [ ] Logical progression

### Literature Review
- [ ] Comprehensive coverage
- [ ] Critical analysis
- [ ] Clear research gap identified
- [ ] Well-organized

### Methodology
- [ ] Replicable procedures
- [ ] Appropriate methods
- [ ] Clear sampling strategy
- [ ] Ethical considerations addressed

### Results
- [ ] Clear presentation
- [ ] Appropriate visuals
- [ ] No interpretation mixed in
- [ ] All data accounted for

### Discussion
- [ ] Results interpreted
- [ ] Literature connected
- [ ] Limitations acknowledged
- [ ] Future directions suggested

### Conclusion
- [ ] Main points summarized
- [ ] No new information
- [ ] Clear contribution stated
- [ ] Strong closing

### References
- [ ] Consistent formatting
- [ ] All sources cited
- [ ] Complete information
- [ ] Current sources

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Introduction too long** - Get to the point quickly
2. **Literature review as list** - Synthesize, don't catalog
3. **Methodology too vague** - Be specific enough to replicate
4. **Results with interpretation** - Save analysis for discussion
5. **Discussion restates results** - Interpret and explain
6. **New info in conclusion** - Everything should appear earlier
7. **Inconsistent citations** - Follow style guide strictly
8. **Poor transitions** - Guide reader through your argument
